# TOEFL iBT Writing Assessment

## Independent Writing Task

**Requirements**: Write an essay based on a prompt in 30 minutes, minimum 300 words.

**Scoring**: 0-5 scaled to 0-30 for the writing section.

### Score 5 (Advanced)

An essay at this level:
- Effectively addresses the topic and task
- Is well organized and well developed, using clearly appropriate explanations, exemplifications, or details
- Displays unity, progression, and coherence
- Displays consistent facility in language use, demonstrating syntactic variety, appropriate word choice, and idiomaticity

### Score 4 (High-Intermediate)

An essay at this level:
- Addresses the topic and task well, but some points may not be fully developed
- Is generally well organized and well developed, using appropriate explanations, exemplifications, or details
- Displays unity, progression, and coherence
- Displays facility in language use, demonstrating syntactic variety and appropriate word choice, but may have minor lexical or grammatical errors

### Score 3 (Low-Intermediate)

An essay at this level:
- Addresses the topic and task but may not fully develop ideas
- Is organized and developed with some details but may lack coherence
- Contains some unity, progression, and coherence
- Displays some language facility but may have noticeable errors in word choice or grammar

### Score 2 (Basic)

An essay at this level:
- May not fully address the topic and task
- Is poorly organized and developed
- Lacks unity, progression, and coherence
- Contains frequent errors in grammar and word choice that interfere with meaning

### Score 1 (Very Basic)

An essay at this level:
- Does not address the topic or task
- Is disorganized
- Contains serious and frequent errors in grammar and word choice
- Little or no meaningful language

## Integrated Writing Task

**Requirements**: Read a passage, listen to a lecture, write a response in 20 minutes summarizing how the lecture relates to the passage.

### Key Skills Assessed

1. **Selection of important information**: Identify key points from both the reading and lecture
2. **Coherence in writing**: Organize response logically
3. **Paraphrase**: Restate ideas in your own words
4. **Connection of ideas**: Show how lecture points relate to or challenge reading points

### Scoring Focus

- Content accuracy (most important)
- Organization
- Language use (grammar and vocabulary)

## Common TOEFL Writing Errors

1. **Word count issues**: writing less than 300 words (independent task)
2. **Off-topic responses**: not addressing the specific prompt
3. **Template overuse**: obvious memorized templates without development
4. **Time management**: not completing within time limits
5. **Lack of examples**: making claims without supporting evidence
