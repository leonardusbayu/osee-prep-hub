# Kurikulum Merdeka — English Language Learning (Indonesia)

## Overview

Kurikulum Merdeka (Merdeka Curriculum) is Indonesia's national curriculum framework introduced in 2022, emphasizing:
- Character development (Profil Pelajar Pancasila)
- Competency-based learning
- Flexibility for schools to adapt content
- Project-based learning

## English Language Learning in Kurikulum Merdeka

### Target Competencies

**Phase E (Grade 11-12 SMA/SMK)**:
- Understanding and creating various spoken and written texts in English
- Using English for academic and professional purposes
- Critical analysis of English-language media

**Phase D (Grade 9-10 SMP/SMA)**:
- Understanding and creating simple spoken and written texts
- Describing experiences, plans, and opinions
- Using English for daily communication in familiar contexts

### Text Types (Phase E)

Students should be able to produce:
1. **Narrative texts**: short stories, personal narratives
2. **Descriptive texts**: describing people, places, objects
3. **Procedural texts**: instructions, how-to guides
4. **Expository texts**: reports, essays, articles
5. **Persuasive texts**: opinion essays, argumentative writing
6. **Formal correspondence**: job applications, business letters

### Assessment Approach

Kurikulum Merdeka uses:
- **Formative assessment**: ongoing, diagnostic, focused on learning process
- **Summative assessment**: end of unit/term, evaluates competency achievement
- **Authentic assessment**: real-world tasks, projects, portfolios

### Grading Scale

Indonesian schools use a 0-100 scale:
- **A (90-100)**: Excellent — exceeds competency standards
- **B (80-89)**: Good — meets standards with minor gaps
- **C (70-79)**: Satisfactory — meets basic competency
- **D (60-69)**: Needs improvement — below competency
- **E (<60)**: Unsatisfactory — significant remediation needed

## Integration with International Tests

### TOEFL ITP
- Used by many Indonesian universities for graduation requirement
- Minimum score: 450-500 (varies by institution)
- Tests: Listening, Structure & Written Expression, Reading

### IELTS
- Required for overseas study (Australia, UK, New Zealand scholarships)
- Minimum band: 6.0-6.5 for undergraduate, 6.5-7.0 for postgraduate
- Tests: Listening, Reading, Writing, Speaking

### TOEIC
- Used by Indonesian companies for recruitment
- Listening & Reading: 10-990 scale
- Speaking & Writing: 0-200 scale

## Teaching Strategies for Indonesian Context

1. **Bilingual approach**: bridge L1 (Bahasa Indonesia) and L2 (English)
2. **Cultural relevance**: use Indonesian contexts in examples
3. **Scaffolding**: provide sentence frames and vocabulary banks
4. **Peer collaboration**: group work to reduce anxiety
5. **Technology integration**: use digital tools for engagement

## Common Challenges for Indonesian English Learners

1. **Pronunciation**: Indonesian phonemes differ from English (no /θ/, /ð/, /ʒ/)
2. **Grammar**: Indonesian has no tense system, articles, or plural markers
3. **Vocabulary**: limited exposure to English outside classroom
4. **Writing structure**: Indonesian rhetorical patterns differ from English
5. **Confidence**: fear of making errors inhibits production
