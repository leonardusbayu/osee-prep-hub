# OSEE Prep Hub — Dream Video Presentation Prompt

## Format
A 3-5 minute product vision video. Magazine-editorial aesthetic throughout. Warm, inspiring, Indonesian-context. The tone is "a beautiful magazine that happens to teach English" — not a tech demo, not a corporate pitch. Think Apple keynote meets The New Yorker meets Indonesian classroom warmth.

---

## OPENING — The Problem (0:00-0:30)

**Visual:** Slow pan across an Indonesian classroom at dusk. A teacher sits at her desk, surrounded by 5 open browser tabs — a practice platform, a grading tool, a WhatsApp group, a spreadsheet, and a PDF textbook. She looks tired. The camera slowly pushes in on her face, lit by the laptop glow.

**Text overlay (magazine masthead style, Georgia serif, gold rule beneath):**
"INDONESIAN ENGLISH TEACHERS DESERVE BETTER"

**Narration (warm, Indonesian-accented English, then switches to Bahasa):**
"1.4 million English teachers in Indonesia. 70 million students. Each teacher juggling 5 platforms, 3 WhatsApp groups, and zero time to actually teach. This is the problem we solve."

**Transition:** The 5 browser tabs collapse into one. The screen goes cream (#F7F5F0). A gold rule draws across the screen left to right.

## ACT 2 — The Four AI Agents (1:00-2:00)

**Visual:** Split screen into 4 quadrants. Each quadrant lights up as its agent is introduced.

### Quadrant 1 — The Curator (top left)
**Visual:** A teacher types: "I have a B1 student targeting IELTS 6.5 in 12 weeks." The Curator agent responds with 3 suggested syllabus items — each with a colored type chip, title, and rationale. The items drag into the week timeline.

**Narration:**
"The Curator. Your syllabus co-author. It knows your student's level, target score, and weak areas. It suggests materials from 4 practice platforms, EduBot, and your own library. Three to five items per turn. You approve, you publish."

### Quadrant 2 — The Coach (top right)
**Visual:** A student opens the Coach chat. Types: "Saya bingung tentang IELTS speaking part 2." The Coach responds in Bahasa Indonesia — a Socratic question, not the answer. A gold "AI TUTOR" kicker sits above the chat. A floating "Ask Coach" button pulses on the student's dashboard.

**Narration:**
"The Coach. A 24/7 Socratic tutor. Patient. Syllabus-aware. When the student is stuck, it switches to Bahasa Indonesia. When they succeed, it celebrates — 'Bagus!' It never gives the answer. It asks one guiding question at a time."

### Quadrant 3 — The Examiner (bottom left)
**Visual:** A student submits an IELTS Task 2 essay. The Examiner responds with a grading report: band 6.5, breakdown by criterion (Task Achievement 6.0, Coherence 7.0, Lexical Resource 6.5, Grammar 6.5), 3 strengths with cited phrases, 3 weaknesses with fixes, and a rewritten paragraph.

**Narration:**
"The Examiner. Rigorous. Official IELTS, TOEFL, and TOEIC rubrics. Band score with breakdown. Three strengths. Three weaknesses. One rewrite. Fair but strict — most Indonesian learners score 5.5 to 6.5 on first attempt, and the Examiner tells them exactly why."

### Quadrant 4 — The Mentor (bottom right)
**Visual:** A student asks: "I want to study abroad in 2 years." The Mentor responds: "With your current pace, you'll hit IELTS 6.5 in 14 weeks. That's enough for an Ausbildung visa in Germany if you also do 200 hours of workplace German. What's your long-term goal — study, work, or personal?"

**Narration:**
"The Mentor. A career coach that thinks in years, not weeks. Year 1: reach the target score. Year 2-3: use English in real contexts. Year 3-5: achieve the career outcome. It connects credentials to job markets. It's honest about timelines."

**Transition:** The 4 quadrants merge into one screen. Text: "FOUR AGENTS. ONE WORKSPACE. 24/7."

## PRODUCTION NOTES

### Visual style
- **Color palette:** Cream background (#F7F5F0), navy ink (#1A1A2E), gold accent (#C9A96E), crimson (#E63946). Student portal scenes use purple accent (#925FE2). Teacher portal scenes use blue accent (#0177FB).
- **Typography:** Georgia serif for headlines/titles/numbers. Inter sans-serif for body/captions/labels. No font below 11pt.
- **Shapes:** Sharp corners (0-4px radius). No rounded bubbles. Magazine flat.
- **Texture:** Flat, high-contrast. No heavy shadows. Subtle 1px borders.
- **Motion:** Slow, deliberate. Gold rules draw left-to-right. Text fades in. No bounce, no spin, no particle effects. Magazine page-turn transitions only.

### Audio
- **Music:** Warm, minimal piano + ambient pad. Indonesian gamelan motif subtly in the background of Act 1 (the problem). Builds to hopeful, aspirational by Act 6.
- **Narration:** Indonesian-accented English. Switches to Bahasa Indonesia for the Coach scene (authentic). Warm, not corporate. Like a teacher telling a story.
- **SFX:** Subtle paper-turn sound on transitions. Soft "stamp" sound when PUBLISH is clicked. Gentle notification chime when a credential is issued.

### Text overlays
- Magazine masthead style: Georgia serif, large, gold rule beneath
- Kicker labels: Helvetica, 11pt, all-caps, letter-spaced, gold or stone color
- Pull quotes: Georgia italic, large, gold rules top + bottom
- Stat cards: Georgia 48pt number + Helvetica 11pt label

### Pacing
- 5:15 total (can be cut to 3:00 for social by dropping Act 5 + Act 6)
- Each act: 30-45 seconds
- No act longer than 45 seconds
- Transitions: 2 seconds max (gold rule draw or page turn)

### Key product URLs to show
- prep.osee.co.id (main portal)
- /verify/:credentialId (employer verification)
- /.well-known/passport-public-key.pem (public key)

### Tone words
- Warm. Not corporate. Not tech-bro. Not "disruptive."
- Indonesian. Jakarta, Surabaya, Bandung — not Silicon Valley.
- Magazine. Editorial. Considered. Not flashy.
- Human. The AI serves the teacher, not the other way around.
- Hopeful. The ending is a teacher with time to teach.

### What NOT to show
- Code. No terminal, no IDE, no JSON.
- Dashboards with 50 metrics. Keep it to 3-4 numbers max per screen.
- Generic "AI" imagery (brains, robots, neural networks). Show the actual product.
- Stock footage of "diverse students smiling at laptops." Show the real UI.
- Buzzwords ("leverage," "synergy," "revolutionize"). Say what it does, plainly.
